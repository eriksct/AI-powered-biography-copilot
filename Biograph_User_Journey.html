1
Awareness & Discovery
Days 1–7
Actions

Finding Biograph

  • Searches "biography writing tools" or "interview transcription for biographers"
  • Discovers Biograph through a colleague's recommendation or online community
  • Visits the landing page, watches the 2-minute demo video
  • Reads feature descriptions: recording, transcription, AI-assisted writing
Landing page Search / Word-of-mouth Demo video
Thoughts & Feelings

"Could this actually solve my workflow?"

Curious but skeptical. Has tried generic transcription tools (Otter, Rev) that didn't understand the biography workflow. Wonders if this is just another tool that won't fit. The promise of going from interview to draft catches attention.

Pain Points

Barriers to Trying

  • Unsure if AI can preserve the subject's authentic voice
  • Worried about data privacy for sensitive life stories
  • No way to test with real material before committing
Trust in AI quality Privacy concerns
Opportunities

Build Trust Early

  • Show a real before/after example (transcript → polished prose)
  • Display security credentials and data handling practices prominently
  • Offer testimonials from other biography writers specifically
Social proof Landing → Signup rate
Key Insight: The #1 concern at this stage isn't price — it's whether the AI will misrepresent their subject's voice. Address this fear head-on with concrete examples.

Reduce time-to-value Signup → First project rate

3
First Interview & Recording
Week 1–2
Actions

Recording the Interview

  • Arrives at subject's home, opens Biograph on laptop
  • One-click start recording — begins interview
  • Sees real-time transcription appearing as they talk
  • Bookmarks key moments during the conversation
  • Pauses/resumes as the subject takes breaks
  • Stops recording after 60–90 minutes (subject tires)
Recording interface Real-time transcription Bookmarking
Thoughts & Feelings

"I can actually be present in the conversation"

This is the "aha moment." The writer maintains eye contact, listens deeply, knowing the platform is capturing everything. Feels liberated from note-taking anxiety. Emotional connection with the subject strengthens.

Peak emotional moment
Pain Points

Real-World Friction

  • Subject's home may have poor WiFi — can transcription work offline?
  • Elderly voices / accents may reduce transcription accuracy
  • Browser-based recording feels less reliable than a dedicated app
  • Fear of losing the recording if something crashes
Connectivity anxiety Accuracy with elderly voices
Opportunities

Build Recording Confidence

  • Offline-capable recording with sync-later transcription
  • Visual confidence indicators during recording ("Audio quality: Good")
  • Auto-save with crash recovery guarantee
  • Low-confidence word highlighting post-transcription
Reliability = trust Recording completion rate
Key Insight: The interview is the highest-stakes moment. If the writer trusts the recording, they'll trust the platform. Reliability here makes or breaks long-term retention.
4
Review & Organize
Week 2–4
Actions

Working with Transcripts

  • Reviews completed transcription in the recordings panel
  • Uses keyword search (Cmd+K) to find mentions of people, places, events
  • Clicks on transcript text to jump to exact audio moment
  • Corrects low-confidence words by listening to the audio
  • Conducts additional interview sessions, building up material
Transcript search Audio playback Recordings list
Actions

Organizing the Story

  • Tags transcript segments by theme (childhood, career, family)
  • Drags story fragments onto the visual timeline
  • Places stories chronologically, handling time jumps
  • Spots gaps — realizes they need to ask about the 1970s in the next session
Timeline view Theme tagging
Thoughts & Feelings

"I'm saving so much time"

The old process meant re-listening to entire recordings. Now, finding a specific story takes seconds. The timeline reveals the life story's shape. Growing excitement as the biography structure emerges from the material.

Pain Points

Organization Complexity

  • Subject told the same story differently across sessions — which version to use?
  • Some stories don't fit neatly into a timeline (recurring themes, feelings)
  • Lots of material from multiple sessions can feel overwhelming
Conflicting versions Information overload
Key Insight: This is where the "70% time reduction on transcript review" promise gets validated. Writers who make it through this stage with a clear structure are very likely to convert to paid.
5
Writing & AI Assistance
Week 3–8
Actions

Drafting the Biography

  • Selects transcript sections and converts to narrative prose with one click
  • Uses the AI rephrasing tool — gets 3 options preserving the subject's voice
  • Applies the conciseness tool to tighten wordy passages
  • Writes directly in the distraction-free editor, interleaving with AI suggestions
  • Hovers over drafted paragraphs to see source transcript links
Text editor AI assistant panel Transcript-to-draft Source linking
Thoughts & Feelings

"The AI actually gets the tone right"

Initially cautious with AI suggestions — accepts some, rejects others. Over time, learns to trust the rephrasing while maintaining editorial control. The blank-page anxiety vanishes because they always have transcript material to start from. Flow state unlocked.

Creative flow
Pain Points

AI Limitations

  • AI occasionally produces generic phrasing that loses the subject's personality
  • First-to-third person conversion sometimes feels mechanical
  • No style consistency check across chapters yet
  • Revision history could be easier to navigate for long documents
Voice authenticity Long-doc management
Opportunities

Deepen AI Partnership

  • Let the AI learn this writer's style after 10,000+ words (Phase 2)
  • Flag inconsistencies between transcript sections automatically
  • Suggest which transcript segments haven't been incorporated yet
  • "Coverage map" showing what percentage of source material is in the draft
Stickiness driver AI feature engagement rate (target: 70%)
Key Insight: The writing phase is where daily engagement is highest and where the platform proves its value. Writers who use transcript-to-draft + rephrasing report 50%+ efficiency gains. This is the core retention loop.
6
Review & Refinement
Week 7–10
Actions

Polishing the Draft

  • Re-reads the full draft, checking flow between chapters
  • Uses source linking to verify accuracy against original transcripts
  • Revisits audio for emotional moments — ensures the tone is right
  • Applies AI conciseness tool to tighten the final text
  • Checks revision history to compare earlier and final versions
Revision history Source verification
Thoughts & Feelings

"I'm confident this is accurate and true"

The source linking feature is unexpectedly powerful here — the writer can trace every claim back to the original words. Confidence builds. Pride in the work emerges. Some anxiety about the subject's reaction.

Pain Points

Gaps in the Polish Workflow

  • No subject review workflow yet — must share drafts manually (email, print)
  • No export to professional formats (PDF, EPUB) in MVP
  • Cross-referencing between chapters is fully manual
Manual export No subject review flow
Opportunities

Close the Loop

  • Read-only sharing link for subjects to review and comment (Phase 3)
  • One-click export to PDF with professional biography templates
  • Automated accuracy report: "100% of facts traced to source audio"
Subject collaboration Accuracy confidence score (target: 90%+)

Journey Summary

🎯 Critical Moments of Truth

  • First recording: Does the writer trust the platform to capture everything?
  • First AI suggestion: Does the rephrasing feel authentic to the subject's voice?
  • First source link click: Can the writer verify accuracy in seconds?
  • Project completion: Was this meaningfully faster than the old way?

🔴 Top Pain Points to Address

  • Recording reliability in low-connectivity environments
  • Transcription accuracy for elderly voices and accents
  • AI voice authenticity — must preserve the subject, not sound generic
  • No native export or subject review workflow in MVP
  • Long-document management as biographies grow past 50,000 words

🟢 Biggest Value Drivers

  • Being present in interviews instead of anxiously note-taking
  • Finding any story moment in seconds, not hours
  • Starting from transcript material — never facing a blank page
  • Source linking for confidence in accuracy
  • Overall time savings of 26–33 hours per project

📊 Key Metrics by Stage

  • Awareness: Landing page → Signup conversion
  • Onboarding: Signup → First project (< 5 min)
  • Recording: Recording completion rate, transcription accuracy
  • Organizing: Search frequency (target: 5+/hr), timeline usage (60%+)
  • Writing: AI feature engagement (70%+), daily active time
  • Delivery: Project completion rate, time-to-first-draft
  • Retention: Second project rate (80%+), NPS (50+), paid conversion (30%+)
